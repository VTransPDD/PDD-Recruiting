Exciting Career Opportunities at the Vermont Agency of Transportation!!

The Vermont Agency of Transportation (VTrans) is seeking to hire self-motivated Civil Engineers, Technitions, and Project managers to join the Highway, Rail Road, and Aviation Programs.

The Team

All dedicated to supporting the VTrans Mission;

“Provide for the safe and efficient movement of people and goods”.

The Role

We are seeking passionate transportation engineers and Project Managers to join our Agency. There are many oppurtunities to be had based on your interests. Potential duties may entail: the collection and analysis of engineering data, the preparation and evaluation of engineering design and construction plans for bridge, railroad, aviation, and roadway projects, construction field inspection, hydraulics analysis, geotechnical and foundation design, materials testing, or traffic engineering.

Bring your solid communication skills to bear on complex problems. Come ready to learn, because our Agency possesses deep institutional, industrial, and transportation engineering knowledge.

    The Skills

    Structures is a fun an exciting group where you will gain a diverse bridge experience. What will you do on day one?

    Frontend

    Ability to read, understand and evaluate engineering plans and specifications. Ability to read and understand technical writing and understand and interpret complex and technical information.

    CADD

    Basic experience with CADD software such as MicroStation, AutoCAD, 3D Modeling Software, or InRoads.

    Hydraulics

    Basic familiarity with hydraulics concepts such as hydrology, fluvial geomorphology, aquatic organism passage, bridge scour, and sediment transport.

    Design

    Understanding of steel and concrete design principals and the ability to understand engineering code.

    Organization

    You delight in the details. You enjoy keeping your work organized and easy to understand and check. You take the extra time to write out all of your assumptions.

    Resourceful

    You won't be asked to fly solo, but you should be resourceful enough to find solutions when you're stuck.

    Self-Taught

    You’ll be surrounded by teammates with deep experience in design, cost estimating, CADD, planning and hydraulics, but we expect to be learning just as much from you

    The Process

    What to expect during the hiring process.

    1. Posting open until January 18, 2019
      2. Due to state recruiting policy, you may not hear anything from us until the position closes.

    2. Early to Mid January: Interview panel reviews applicants
    3. Mid to Late Jan: Selected candidates offered interviews
    4. Mid Jan to Feb: First interviews
    5. Mar: Second interviews (If necessary)
    6. Mar: Offer
    Interviews

    Interviews will be panel style, with each panelist asking a few questions. You should expect to take up an entire hour, but 90 minutes have been allotted to provide for overrun.

    The panel will ask you questions based on our analysis of the core competencies required for this position.

    There will also be a short quiz to evaluate your critical thinking ability.

    How to Apply

    1. Select the appropriate job class for your skill level:
      • Civil Engineer I/II
      • Civil Engineer III/IV
      • Civil Engineer V/VI
    2. Create an Account in VTHR
    3. Apply for the job
      • Answer the Questionnaire*

    Important: Fill out all required education and work experience in the application, but Be sure to attach a well formatted resume and cover letter as well.

    The output from the VTHR system is very difficult to read, and your skills and experience will be communicated more effectively in your resume.

    *These questions will be used by the system to determine your eligibility for the position.
