Become Vermont's next Civil Engineering Expert

The Vermont Agency of Transportation (VTrans) in Montpelier is seeking to hire a self motivated enrtry level Civil Engineer to join the Structures and Hydraulics Section.

The Team

Located in the Project Delivery Bureau, the Structures and Hydraulics Section is dedicated to supporting the VTrans Mission;

“Provide for the safe and efficient movement of people and goods”. We are proud to emphasize innovation in design, accelerated bridge construction, and alternative contracting techniques used to accelerate project delivery and underscore our commitment to quality, resiliency, and public engagement.

The Role

We are seeking a passionate civil engineer to join our section. Duties entail the collection and analysis of engineering data, and the preparation and evaluation of engineering design and construction plans and specifications. Based on what skills you are interested in developing there are opurtunities to work with the scoping and hydaulics team. Bring your solid communication skills to bear on complex problems. Come ready to learn, because our Agency possesses deep institutional, industrial, and transportation engineering knowledge.

The Hydraulics Unit is a small but dynamic group of professionals with the primary function of supporting the design and permitting of bridges and culverts to result in infrastructure that is compatible with Vermont’s rivers and streams. This involvement in the design process helps to ensure alignment with VTrans’ strategic goal to, “provide a safe and resilient transportation system in an environmentally responsible manner”. The Hydraulics Unit is trained in the specialty areas of hydrology, fluvial geomorphology, aquatic organism passage, bridge scour, and sediment transport.

This is a fun and exciting place to work. The role will be immersed in a diverse multidisiplinary bridge experience. You will have the chance to perform geotechnical and foundation design, roadway design, traffic engineering, hydraulics, and scoping to name a few.

The Skills

Frontend

Ability to read, understand and evaluate engineering plans and specifications. Ability to read and understand technical writing and understand and interpret complex and technical information.

CADD

Basic experience with CADD software such as Microstation, AutoCADD, 3D Modeling Software, or InRoads.

Hydraulics

Basic familiarity with hydraulics concepts such as hydrology, fluvial geomorphology, aquatic organism passage, bridge scour, and sediment transport.

Design

Understanding of steel and concrete design principals and the ability to understand engineering code.

Organization

You delight in the details. You enjoy keeping your work organized and easy to understand and check. You take the extra time to write out all of your assumptions.

Resourceful

You won't be asked to fly solo, but you should be resourceful enough to find solutions when you're stuck.

Self-Taught

You’ll be surrounded by teammates with deep experience in design, cost estimating, CADD, planning and hydraulics, but we expect to be learning just as much from you

The Tasks

Structures is a fun an exciting group where you will gain a diverse bridge experience. What will you do on day one?

Bridge Design

At VTrans, bridge engineers are involved with all aspects of a project. This means you will get the opurtunity to do geotechnical foundation design, roadway design, and traffic design. Cost estimating will also be a part of your job duties. Grow yourself to be a Project Manager if that is where your interests are!

Scoping and Hydraulics

The structures section has a dedicated scoping unit and an embeded hydraulics unit. Oppurtunities to work in these groups are available to applicants with interest in those areas.

Modeling/MicroStation

VTrans is just getting into the world of 3D modeling. Perhaps you could lead the effort and become an expert in this subject.
